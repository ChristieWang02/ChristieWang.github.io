    Design exam

    Description of activity:

    A one hour exam, testing students on the unit of Design within the Stage 6 Preliminary course. The exam is broken up into two sections, the first features a range of multiple choice questions whilst the second requires students to use short responses to answer the questions.

    Instructions:

  • Have a pen/ pencil and paper ready beside you.

    Task analysis:

    This task is designed to be used as a form of summative assessment in order to collect student data regarding their overall understanding of the topic area. This activity addresses outcomes P1.1 (describes the elements and principles of design and uses them in a variety of applications) and P1.2 (identifies the functional and aesthetic requirements and features of a range of textile items) within the preliminary Textiles and Design curriculum (NESA, 2013b). As students are challenged with an exam which tests them on their ability to apply their knowledge from the unit (Armstrong, 2010), they are actively comprehending texts, using their word knowledge and creating texts support the literacy general capabilities (Australian curriculum, n.d.a). They are supported with additional resources which they can study voluntarily which covers all the content within this topic area (AITSL, n.d.).
