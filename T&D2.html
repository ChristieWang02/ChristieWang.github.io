    Activity 2:

    Australian Textile, Clothing, Footwear and Allied Industries - Company research task

    Description of activity:

    A series of questions asking students to investigate into a company (of their choosing) that works within the Australian Textile, Clothing, Footwear and Allied Industries.

    Instructions:

  • Select any company situated within the Australian Textile, Clothing, Footwear and Allied Industries.
  • Investigate your chosen company by answering the questions in the research task template provided.

    • Regarding the template:

  • Open the research task template document.
  • Make your own copy of the document.
  • Name the copy" - insert full name - research task".
  • Answer the questions in YOUR COPY OF THE DOCUMENT.
  • You will be given time in this class to complete the task. The remaining must be completed as homework to be done by the end of the week (Sunday).
  • Submit your completed research task into the appropriate google classroom tile.

    • Task analysis:

    This task is designed to encourage students to delve deeper into companies situated within the ACTFAI. This task addresses outcomes P5.1 (examines the status of the Australian Textile, Clothing, Footwear and Allied Industries within the global context) and P6.1 (identifies and appreciates the factors that contribute to the quality and value of textiles in society) within the preliminary Textiles and Design curriculum (NESA, 2013b). This task encourages higher order thinking as students are required to analyse the company of their choice, prompted by the questions they are provided with in their scaffolding (Armstrong, 2010). As they begin to draw connections, students are actively engaging in research which involve comprehending texts, word knowledge and creating texts which ultimately support literacy general capabilities (Australian curriculum, n.d.a). By assigning this task to students, they are able to get a better grasp of ACTFAI through their own research as they relate the content to their surroundings, whilst having the resources to support their research if they need (AITSL, n.d.).
