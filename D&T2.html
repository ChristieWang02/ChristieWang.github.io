Content Area:

Managing the production process

Outcomes:

  • P5.1 uses a variety of management techniques and tools to develop design projects

    • Resources:

    Construction process example

  • To help you visualise the process of building a jewellery box, you can use paddle pop sticks. If would like to use paddle pop sticks, ask the teacher and they can provide you with some.

    • Activity 2:

    Creating a Gant chart

    Description of activity:

    Students will learn to visualise the production process of a jewellery box and create a Gant chart that reflects this process.

    Instructions:

  • Take a look at this small jewellery box.
  • Try to visualise how you would make this jewellery box in the workshop from just pieces of timber.
  • Create a table which breaks down every step of the production process for this box.
  • Be as detailed as you can - think about the tools, techniques and processes you will need to use to make this box.
  • Submit your Gant chart in the appropriate google classroom tile.

    • Task analysis:

    This task is designed to encourage students to consider the methods and processes in the construction process for an item whilst providing them with the opportunity to familiarise themselves with the planning stages of design projects. As a result, this activity focuses more on student's ability to understand and apply their knowledge in this area (lower order thinking) as opposed to creating, evaluating or analysing (Armstrong, 2010). This activity addresses outcome P5.1 (uses a variety of management techniques and tools to develop design projects) within the preliminary Design and Technology curriculum (NESA, 2013a). Students will be required to predict the steps in production for the jewellery box which establishes a challenging learning goal (AITSL, n.d.), especially for students with no previous experience in the subject area/ workshop. However, support is given to students in the form of worked examples, paddle pop sticks (tangible objects) which can help visualise the production process in conjunction with written instructions to differentiate teaching strategies in order to support student learning needs (AITSL, n.d.). Finally, this task will require students to use industry specific terms which aligns with the literacy general capabilities (Australian curriculum, n.d.a).
